A significant portion of §lang_0 syntax is reconstructed. The few attestations we have access to do not provide us with more than basic constructions. As such, a great portion of this chapter has been reconstructed through comparison with contemporary languages in its vicinity, as well as the syntax of later Carite.

Verb Phrase

The verb is the central element of any clause and, without exception, must be present in all clauses. A verb phrase is the combination of a verb and its objects, of which the number and type differ according to the type of verb, predicate complements and additional adverbials.

Objects

As mentioned before, the number and type of objects depend on the type of the verb. In the case of an intransitive verb, the object is excluded, while in the case of a transitive verb, it may be required.
