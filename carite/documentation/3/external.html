Consonants

By the fifth century, two key processes in the evolution of Latin had taken place: the palatalisation of velars before front velars (as well as /kj/) and the spirantisation, voicing and degemination of plosives. For example, °ENEMY° reflects lat@inimīcus.

°BREINA°, reflecting the Late lat@brigna, shows the Late Latin realisation of /gn/ as /ɲ/; this sound was alien to the §lang_3 speakers, who unpacked it into the cluster /nj/.
