Nominal Morphology

Developments

Very few notorious developments happened in terms of nominal morphology from §lang_2 to §lang_3. The two most important changes are the loss of the locative as distinct case, as well as the full merger of the acrostatic nominals into thematic neuters.

Degrees of comparison

With both comparatives and superlatives inducing i-umlaut, thematic adjectives occasionally showed stem alteration if in another degree than the positive. This alteration affected the stem regardless of gender, leading to some adjectives having three distinct stems (and some not undergoing any alteration at all). Iotic adjectives, on the other hand, never showed this alteration due to already having been affected by i-umlaut in their positive stem.

Comparative

Due to its unique inflection pattern, the comparative degree does not distinguish between adjectival and substantival usage. However, the suffixes can vary wildly due to the separation of delta and alpha variants, as well as the added possibility of being an Exon variant. Luckily, the choice of comparative suffixes was perfectly predictable from the inflection paradigm of the positive adjective. While iotic adjectives are assumed to originally have made a distinction between delta and alpha comparatives, this distinction must have been levelled out gradually due to all iotic positive adjectives declining as if they were delta.
