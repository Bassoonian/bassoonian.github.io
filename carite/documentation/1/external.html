External Interactions

Situation

In the roughly 450 years of timespan between §lang_0 and what is often considered to be the ending point of §lang_1, the Carites moved significantly further west, presumably crossing the Pyrenees early on. Considering there are no attestations, contact with external languages is generally used as a reference in an attempt to situate the migration pattern, although this method is not waterproof.

Ancient Greek

Extensive contact with Ancient Greek continued, although we may no longer date any Doric loans from this stage. It is likely that this is the result of the departure from the seaside, which sealed them off the Mediterranean trade network.
