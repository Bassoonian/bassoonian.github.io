§lang_1's consonant inventory is reconstructed in extremely similar fashion to §lang_0's, with the only difference being the new phoneme /x/ and the lack of /z/. [ɱ] and [ŋ] remain as allophones before labiodentals and velars, respectively, while [ŋ] also surfaces for /ng/ in its entirety before sonorants or plosives.

With the introduction of phonemic nasal vowels, the reconstructed vowel inventory of §lang_1 is significantly larger than its precedessor. These nasal vowels are always long, with a short allophone word-finally in polysyllables. [y] and [ø], previously limited to a marginal and allophonic status, respectively, are now both fully phonemic due to the word-final shortening in polysyllables.

While some prefer to consider these plain sequences of a vowel and a glide, the communis opinio reconstructs several diphthongs for §lang_1. These consist (with one exception) of a (short) oral vowel followed by a glide. The exception is /õi/, which occurs only once, and may have been pronounced [õĩ̯].

Phonotactics

||PHONOTACTICS||

Suprasegmentals

Stress fell on the first syllable of the stem - as such, prefixes such as the augment were never stressed.

Morphophonology

Besides an active regressive voicing assimilation filter, we may find the collapse of two identical vowels into a single long vowel of the same quality, i.e. |V₁V₁(ː)| /V₁ː/.
