Original Text

Kyrshaxis, the exalted one, divine shepherd of man, king of kings, do hereby decree: the state of Hejkos shall be punished for the part it played in the war. Each year on the summer solstice Hejkos shall tithe one-eighth of its gold, a great gross bushels of grain or drink, and two regiments of trained men for use in Kyrshaxis' military. Failure to deliver in a timely manner shall result in the lands being ransacked and reparations increased.

Translation

T́irápasam says “It is T́irápasam’s grand kingdom, and Ikásaam is king”. They will attack (the) kingdom (and I do not want this to happen), so Ikásaam wants to wage war on them. They will not attack (and I want this to happen), so I say “We have one month to pay them many treasures of debt”. But they won’t pay the many treasures of debt, so they will take our treasures (and I do not want this to happen).

Translation

Name yourself, Ggilzagzi. I heard the high spirit that presides over the text of knowledge has accepted the member’s knowledge: It is a good day to punish Aiggazi. Before the two armies could gather in the battlefield with Ggilzagzi’s soldiers, they bought a lot of alcohol with one-eighth of their money. They do not rely on the country’s stolen things.
